は

は marks the subject or topic of a sentence.

は here is pronounced "wa".

Examples:

私は学生です。
I am a student.

メアリーさんはアメリカ人です。
Mary is an American.

Sentences without subject are very common, as subject can be understood from context.

の

の　connects two nouns.

noun の noun

restriction の main idea

Examples:

日本の学校
a Japanese school

私のお母さん
My mother

あなたの電話番号
your phone number

の can also replace a noun when it follows an adjective.

This only works when it is very obvious what is being referred to, in order to avoid repetition.

Examples:

私は黒いセーターを持っています。赤いの持っています。
I have a black sweater. I also have a red one.

ケーキが沢山あります。好きなの持っていってください。
We have lots of cakes. Please take the ones you like.

も

も means "too" or "as well"

も replaces the subject particle. も　does not replace time or location particles.

Examples:

たけしさんは学生です。ゆいさんも学生です。
Takeshi is a student. Yui is also a student.

も can only be used in cases of "Item A is this, item B is this too".
も is not used to mean "Item A is this as well as this".

も　is also used when two or more people do the same activity, or when one person buys, sees, or eats more than one thing.

Examples:

メアリーさんは靴を買いました。かばんも買いました。
Mary bought shoes. She bought a bag, too.

先週京都に行きました。大阪にも行きました。
Last weekend, I went to Kyoto. I went to Osaka, too.

ね・よ

ね and よ are used to add emphasis to statements.

ね is used to seek the listener's confirmation or agreement.
This is like adding "right?" to a statement.

よ is used to tell the listener that you are fully confident in your statement.
This is like "Let me tell you" or "You better believe it".

Examples:

あなたの専攻は文学ですね。
Your major is literature, right?

豚カツは魚じゃないですよ。
Tonkatsu is definitely not fish.

を

を marks the direct object of a sentence.

The direct object is the object directly involved in, or directly effected by the events in the sentence.
を is pronounced "oh".

Examples:

コーヒーを飲みます。
I drink coffee.

音楽を聞きます。
I listen to music.

で

で indicates where the verb action takes place.

Examples:

図書館で本を読みます。
I read a book at the library.

家で昼ご飯を食べます。
I eat lunch at home.

に

に has many meanings.

Goal of movement

For verbs like 行く、来る、帰る

Examples:

学校に行きます。
I go to school.

家に帰ります。
I return home.

Time

Only needed for:
-Days of the week
-Months of the year
-Numerical time expressions

に can optionally be used for:
-Parts of the day (morning, night)
-週末

に is NOT used for:
-Time expressions relative to the present time (tommorrow, today)
-Expressions describing regular intervals (every day)
-いつ

Examples:

日曜日に京都に行きます。
I will go to Kyoto on Sunday.

十一時に帰ります。
I will return home at 11 o'clock.

へ

Goal of movement

へ can be used in place of に for verbs like 行く、来る、帰る。

へ is pronounced "eh".

Examples:

学校へ行きません。
I will not go to school.

家へ帰ります。
I will return home.

と

と　is used for:

Connecting two nouns together (and)

Examples:

日本語と英語を話します。
I speak Japanese and English.

京都と大阪に行きました。
I went to Kyoto and Osaka.

Describing with whom you do something

Examples:

メアリーさんはソラさんと韓国に行きます。
Mary will go to Korea with Sora.

公園で子供と遊びます。
I play with my kids are the park.

が

が is used for:

Giving emphasis to the subject of a sentence

Examples:

ロバートさんが沖縄に行きました。
Robert went to Okinawa.

In this sentence, both speaker and listener know someone went to Okinawa, but the speaker knows that it was Robert specifically, and wants to share that information.

Indicating that a question word is the subject of the sentence
Answers to these questions should also have が in place of は

Examples:

どのクラスが面白いですか。
Which of your classes is interesting?

日本語のクラスが面白いです。
Japanese class is.
